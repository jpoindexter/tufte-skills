# Cognitive Style and Analytical Communication

**Source:** Beautiful Evidence (BE), 2006, Chapters 4–6; Seeing with Fresh Eyes (SFE), 2020

## Overview
Tufte coins the phrase "the cognitive style of PowerPoint" to name something broader than a software problem: the systematic degradation of analytical thinking that results from fragmenting evidence into bullet-point sequences. The core argument is that the format of presentation is not neutral — it actively shapes the quality of reasoning that can be expressed and received. Bullet lists can only communicate three logical relationships. Sentences can communicate dozens. A slide can carry 40 words. A single page of the Physicians' Desk Reference carries the information-equivalent of 54 slides. The remedy is not better slides — it is written reports, integrated evidence, and the sentence as the fundamental unit of analysis.

## §1. The Cognitive Style of PowerPoint — Named Deficiencies

"PowerPoint is presenter-oriented, not content-oriented, not audience-oriented." (BE)

Tufte's complete list of structural deficiencies (BE):

1. Foreshortening of evidence and thought
2. Low spatial resolution
3. Intensely hierarchical single-path structure as the model for organizing every type of content
4. Breaking up narratives and data into slides and minimal fragments
5. Rapid temporal sequencing of thin information rather than focused spatial analysis
6. Conspicuous chartjunk and PP Phluff
7. Branding of slides with logotypes
8. Preoccupation with format not content
9. Incompetent designs for data graphics and tables
10. A smirky commercialism that turns information into a sales pitch and presenters into marketeers

**The core structural problem:** Tufte argues in *Beautiful Evidence* that temporal sequencing makes it hard to grasp context and evaluate relationships; visual reasoning works far more effectively when all relevant evidence sits adjacent in space, within a single eyespan.

## §2. The Bullet List — Three and Only Three Logical Relationships

Bullet lists can communicate exactly three logical relationships:
1. **Sequence** — first to last in time
2. **Priority** — least to most important
3. **Set membership** — belonging to a category

"A list can show only one of those relationships at a time." (BE)

"As analysis becomes more causal, multivariate, comparative, evidence-based, and resolution-intense, the more damaging the bullet list becomes." (BE)

**What the bullet list cannot express:**
- Causality
- Conditionality ("if X, then Y")
- Exception ("X is true, except when Y")
- Mechanism ("X causes Y through mechanism Z")
- Comparison with quantified differences
- Any two-dimensional relationship

**Example:** Three bullets — "increase market share 25%," "increase profits 30%," "increase new-product introductions to 10/year" — could represent at least five completely different causal models. The bullet list cannot distinguish between them. The sentence can.

## §3. The Sentence as the Fundamental Unit of Analysis

"An effective methodology for making such statements is the sentence, with subjects and predicates, nouns and verbs, agents and their effects." (BE)

The sentence is the minimum unit that can express:
- Agency (who acts)
- Direction (what they do to what)
- Conditionality (when and under what constraints)
- Causality (why it produces the effect)
- Quantified comparison (how much vs. what)

**Richard Feynman's textbooks:** 3 volumes, 1,800 pages, describing all of physics. No bullets. Only 2 levels of hierarchy. The contrast with physics conference slides (which use bullets) is direct — the slides cannot carry the content the books carry.

**George Orwell's principle applied:** "PowerPoint becomes ugly and inaccurate because our thoughts are foolish, but the slovenliness of PowerPoint makes it easier for us to have foolish thoughts." (BE, adapting Orwell's "Politics and the English Language")

## §4. Information Density Comparison

| Format | Characters | Density (chars/sq in) |
|---|---|---|
| Physicians' Desk Reference (one page) | 13,600 | 168 |
| Boeing PP slides (Columbia report) | 630 | 7 |
| 1,460 PP internet slides (median) | 250 | 3 |
| PP textbook slides (median) | 98 | 1 |
| Content-free slides | 0 | 0 |

"The PP slide format has the worst signal/noise ratio of any known method of communication on paper or computer screen." (BE)

"A single page of the Physicians' Desk Reference shows 54 typical PP slide-equivalents of information." (BE)

**The reading/listening asymmetry:** Readers process text at 300–1,000 words per minute. Speakers present at ~100–150 words per minute. The audience can read the slide content in 8–30 seconds; the presenter then speaks for 3–5 minutes about 40 words of content. The format wastes the reader's capacity and fills the deficit with repetition, platitude, and decoration.

## §5. What Good Analytical Communication Looks Like

**The six requirements (from BE Principles of Analytical Design):**

1. **Show comparisons** — the fundamental analytical act is "compared to what?" Every display must make the comparison explicit and spatial.

2. **Show causality** — name the agents, mechanisms, and directions. Passive voice ("revenue increased") is always inferior to active causality ("new customer acquisition through channel X increased revenue").

3. **Show multivariate data** — the world is multivariate. Any display showing only 1–2 variables at a time is structurally incapable of supporting real analysis.

4. **Integrate evidence modes** — "Words, numbers, pictures, diagrams, graphics, charts, tables belong together." (BE) They must appear together, not in separate sections.

5. **Document thoroughly** — named author, affiliation, data sources, measurement scales, assumptions, sponsor, date. Undocumented evidence is inherently suspect.

6. **Let content drive everything** — Better content is the single most effective upgrade any presentation can receive, Tufte argues. No design technique or visual embellishment can salvage weak substance.

## §6. The 4-Pager Format — The Remedy

"For serious presentations, replace PP with word-processing or page-layout software." (BE)

**The 4-pager:** A3 paper folded in half to make 4 pages. One piece of paper shows:
- Up to 60,000 characters of words and numbers
- Images at 1,200 dpi resolution
- Detailed tables comparable to sports pages
- "1,000 sparklines showing 500,000 numbers" (BE)

"That one piece of paper shows the content-equivalent of 50 to 250 typical PP slides." (BE)

**The presentation procedure:**
1. Distribute the 4-pager at the start of the talk
2. Allow 5–10 minutes of silent reading
3. Provide a guided analysis and discussion
4. Readers process 3× faster than speakers can talk — let them read

## §7. The Columbia Case Study as Cognitive-Style Failure

The Boeing slides presented to NASA managers before the Challenger launch were "a foreshortened presentation of evidence" that exemplify every cognitive-style failure simultaneously:

| Cognitive-style failure | How it appeared |
|---|---|
| Bullet fragmentation | 6 levels of hierarchy to express 11 phrases |
| Punning | "Significant" used with 5 incompatible technical meanings |
| Effects without causes | Outcome words without agents or mechanisms |
| Information hierarchy mismatch | Critical finding buried at level 5, last bullet |
| Low density | 630 characters over the whole slide |
| Context suppression | 640× exceedance of test-database bounds not clearly quantified |

The Columbia Accident Investigation Board found, as Tufte cites in *Beautiful Evidence*, that the slide's structure — its headings, layout, and bullet hierarchy — served to reinforce what managers already assumed rather than surface what was dangerous or unknown. Critical uncertainties never made it up the information chain.

## §8. What Written Reports Preserve That Slides Destroy

| Written report | Slide presentation |
|---|---|
| Sentences preserve agency and causality | Bullets strip agents and mechanisms |
| Persistent: reader can return, re-read, annotate | Ephemeral: gone when slide advances |
| Navigable: reader controls reading path | Sequential: presenter controls path |
| High density: 168 chars/sq in possible | Low density: 1–7 chars/sq in typical |
| Integrated: text, data, images on same page | Segregated: one element per slide |
| Self-documenting: full sentences are their own context | Context-dependent: bullets require speaker to decode |
| Citable and attributable | Often anonymous |

**The Return to Flight Task Group (NASA):** The group found, as Tufte documents in *Beautiful Evidence*, that critical engineering decisions were being recorded only in PowerPoint slides and emails rather than formal reports. The Task Group concluded that such presentations should never substitute for — or even supplement — proper engineering documentation.

## §9. Conway's Law Applied to Presentations

Presentation structure tends to mirror the organizational structure that produced it — not the logical structure of the content. When a presentation has 7 sections because there are 7 organizational units, not 7 logical components of the argument, Conway's Law is operating.

**Diagnostic question:** Is the structure of this presentation determined by the logic of the evidence, or by the org chart of the people who produced it?

## §10. Self-Check Before Any Presentation

| Question | Red flag if... |
|---|---|
| Can the key claims be expressed as complete sentences with agents and effects? | No — bullets only, no sentences |
| Is the most important comparison visible within a single eyespan? | No — split across slides |
| Is the causally relevant variable on a visual axis? | No — or not shown at all |
| Are all data shown, including null cases? | No — only confirming cases |
| Is the author named and the data source cited? | No |
| Could the audience read the full content in 10 minutes? | No — too thin to read, too dense to skim |
| Would this evidence survive a hostile reviewer? | Uncertain — stop, check for cherry-picking |

## §11. Key Maxims

- "PowerPoint is presenter-oriented, not content-oriented, not audience-oriented." (BE)
- "Information stacked in time makes it difficult to understand context and evaluate relationships." (BE)
- "The PP slide format has the worst signal/noise ratio of any known method of communication on paper or computer screen." (BE)
- "The most effective way to improve a presentation is to get better content." (BE)
- "Words, numbers, pictures, diagrams, graphics, charts, tables belong together." (BE)
- "The principles of analytical design are derived from the principles of analytical thinking." (BE)
- "Analytical presentations ultimately stand or fall depending on the quality, relevance, and integrity of their content." (BE)
